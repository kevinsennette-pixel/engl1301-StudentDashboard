A New Chapter in Richmond Education

In 2025, Lamar CISD opened Tomas High School, fundamentally changing the educational landscape for thousands of students in Richmond and Rosenberg, Texas. This project explores the question: How did the construction of Tomas High School transform the educational experience and daily life for students and families who previously attended Foster High School?

Through interviews and analysis of enrollment data and attendance boundaries, this interactive dashboard reveals the impact of opening a new high school in an established community.

What This Means

The redistricting resulted in approximately 500 students moving from Foster to Tomas. According to interviews, Foster had been operating at 125% capacity before Tomas opened.

The Overcrowding Crisis at Foster

By 2025, Foster High School was operating at 125% capacity, with 2,500 students in a building designed for 2,000. The strain was visible in every aspect of school life.

Hallway Crowding

  • Students had to line up at doors before bell rang
  • Risk of tardiness if not first out the door
  • Claustrophobic conditions between classes
  • "Always trying not to bump into somebody"

Temporary Solutions

  • Portable classrooms added near bus porch
  • Good AC, but flooding issues when it rained
  • Puddles made it difficult to access portables
  • Students had to navigate between inside/outside

Facility Issues

  • Constant cockroaches and mice in building
  • Old building with upkeep problems
  • Restroom cleanliness issues
  • Infrastructure strained by overcapacity

The Financial Reality

Building Tomas High School required massive financial investment that impacted the entire Lamar CISD district. The construction was part of a 2020 bond package that included not just the high school, but an entire "family of schools"‚Äîelementary, middle, and high school‚Äîto serve the rapidly developing area.

Initial Investment

  • November 2020: Bond package approved
  • ~$1 billion in bonds sought from Texas and investors
  • Tens of millions just for land acquisition
  • Built entire family of schools (elementary through high school)

District-Wide Cuts

  • All high schools: 25% budget reduction
  • Funds redirected to Tomas construction
  • Even with cuts, Tomas felt financially constrained
  • District still needed to borrow additional money

Long-Term Impact

  • Financial recovery estimated: 5-10 years
  • Not a short-term investment like a car
  • Bought land + built infrastructure permanently
  • Must wait for return on investment

Impact on Foster High School

Foster faced a double impact: budget cuts combined with decreased enrollment meant fewer resources per program.

"This is going to set you back probably five to ten years. It's a really big investment. This is not like, oh, I bought a car and I could trade it in. No, you bought land and then you built up the school. You're going to have to wait to get your investment back."

‚Äî Isam Abawadeh, Foster HS Class of 2025

"I think since Tomas took so much funding and students from Foster, the school has a possibility to go into the red in the coming years."
